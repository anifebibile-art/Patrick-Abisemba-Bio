"My Journey: From Resilience to Leadership." 🌍

The Path of Displacement

Born in the DRC, Ildephonse spent over a decade in refugee camps across Tanzania and Malawi. In a world where higher education felt like a "wild dream" due to legal constraints and displacement, he remained focused on his studies despite a future that seemed uncertain.

A New Beginning at Telfer

His life changed in 2017 when he was selected to study at the University of Ottawa. While the transition brought culture shock and loneliness, he leaned into the campus community and resources, graduating in 2022 with a Bachelor of Commerce in Finance. This experience instilled in him a deep sense of professional ethics and a lifelong commitment to giving back.

"There is always a light at the end of the tunnel. What truly matters is your willingness to be open and ask for help." -Ildephonse Mmangyo

The Twaweza Initiative 🤝

Swahili for "Together, We Can"

Launched in mid-2020 during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, Twaweza began as a response to the isolation and mental health challenges faced by immigrants and refugees. What started as a simple group chat to stay connected evolved into a vital mission to ensure no newcomer has to navigate the Canadian landscape alone.

Financial Education

Providing newcomers with the practical knowledge needed to manage money, build credit, and understand the Canadian banking system.

Employment Readiness

Equipping refugees with the tools and networking skills required to successfully enter and thrive in the Canadian workforce.

Tax Assistance

Helping families and individuals navigate the complexities of annual tax filing to ensure they access the benefits they are entitled to.

"Twaweza became a symbol of our shared determination to get through difficult times as one. We no longer limit ourselves to pandemic support, but offer a roadmap for achieving full financial and social success in Canada." -Ildephonse Mmangyo
