May 13–17, 2024 | 91st Acfas Congress

Host: University of Ottawa | The Largest Academic Gathering in the Francophone World

In May 2024, the University of Ottawa served as the epicenter for the 91st Acfas Congress—a historic event marking the first edition held outside of Quebec in more than a decade. Under the theme "Mobilizing Knowledge in French," the congress brought together over 6,000 delegates from 30 countries to bridge the gap between academic research and public impact.

"Science-Moi!" & Public Engagement

Patrick participated in this landmark initiative designed to bring scientific discovery to the streets. The program featured "Ma thèse en 180 secondes" (My Thesis in 180 Seconds), where elite PhD researchers were challenged to distill complex global issues into three minutes of compelling public discourse.

Contributing to Colloquium 401 (Development and Functioning of Persons and Communities), Patrick presented research supervised by Christine Gibb. His work addresses a critical gap in humanitarian discourse:

The Human Security Framework & Gender-Based Violence

  • Critical Focus: Evaluating Gender-Based Violence (GBV) in refugee camps, specifically sexual assault, forced marriage, and systemic domestic violence.
  • Analytical Framework: Utilizing Human Security theory to bridge the gap between international security rhetoric and the lived experiences of local actors.
  • Key Advocacy: Challenging the classification of refugee camps as "safe" zones and emphasizing the necessity of listening to local voices to protect individuals from systemic violence.

Professional Experience

Education & Community Leadership Trainer

Patrick is proudly affiliated with the Jesuit Refugee Service (JRS) in Malawi. In this role, he has dedicated himself to empowering displaced populations through education and specialized leadership training.

  • Community Empowerment: Leading workshops to develop local leaders within refugee communities.
  • Educational Advocacy: Coordinating programs that provide essential skills and learning opportunities to those in transition.
  • On-the-Ground Impact: Bridging the gap between international humanitarian aid and the daily needs of refugee families.

Documenting East African Heritage

Beyond his academic research, Patrick has a passion for documenting the rich history and landscape of East Africa. He excels at translating complex socio-political history into narratives that celebrate the beauty of the region.

"Exploring the Beauty of Tanzania: People and Culture"

In this work, Patrick provides a detailed overview of Tanzania's geography, culture, and governance. He showcases the country's vibrant heritage, making the complex history of its people and land accessible to a global audience.
